Sets the filter coefficients for a resonance at {\itshape frequency} (in Hz). 

This method determines the filter coefficients corresponding to two complex-\/conjugate poles with the given {\itshape frequency} (in Hz) and {\itshape radius} from the z-\/plane origin. If {\itshape normalize} is true, the filter zeros are placed at z = 1, z = -\/1, and the coefficients are then normalized to produce a constant unity peak gain (independent of the filter {\itshape gain} parameter). The resulting filter frequency response has a resonance at the given {\itshape frequency}. The closer the poles are to the unit-\/circle ({\itshape radius} close to one), the narrower the resulting resonance width.
